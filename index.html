Jenderal TNI Anumerta Ahmad Yani

The man who rejecting the formation of the country's fifth generation from G30S/PKI



Here's a time line of
Jenderal Anumerta Ahmad Yani:

  • 1922 - Born in Purworejo, Central Java, Hindia Belanda
  • 1927 - Move in Batavia with family
  • 1940 - Leave high school to conscription at Hindia Belanda era
  • 1942 - Moved back to Central Java because of the arrival of the Japanese army
  • 1943 - Joined PETA(Pembela Tanah Air)
  • 1949 - Managed to defend Magelang from the Dutch troops
  • 1952 - He was called back into action against Darul Islam, a rebel group seeking to establish a theocracy in Indonesia. To deal with this rebel group, Yani formed a special forces group called The Bull Raiders
  • 1955 - Yani left for the United States to study at Command and General Staff College, Fort Leavenworth, Kansas
  • 1956 - Yani was transferred to Army Headquarters in Jakarta where he became a member of the General staff for Abdul Haris Nasution. At Army Headquarters, Yani served as Logistics Assistant to the Army Chief of Staff before becoming Deputy Army Chief of Staff for Organization and Personnel
  • 1958 - He ordered Operation 17 August against the Revolutionary Government of the Republic of Indonesia in West Sumatra. His troops succeeded in recapturing Padang and Bukittinggi, and this success led to him being promoted to 2nd deputy Army chief of staff on 1 September 1962, and then Army Chief of staff on 13 November 1963 (automatically a member of the cabinet), replacing General Nasution
  • 1960 - Yani and Nasution procrastinated when ordered by Sukarno on 31 May 1965 to prepare a plan to arm the people, and rejecting the formation of the country's fifth generation from G30S/PKI
  • 1965 - The September 30th Movement attempted to kidnap seven members of the Army general staff. A team of about 200 people surrounded Yani's house on Jalan Latuhahary No. 6 on the outskirts of Jakarta Menteng, Central Jakarta. Yani's body, and that of other victims, was exhumed on October 4, and all were given a state funeral the following day, before being buried at the Heroes Cemetery in Kalibata. On the same day, Yani and his colleagues were officially declared Heroes of the Revolution by Presidential Decree No. 111/KOTI/1965 and were posthumously promoted from Lieutenant General to 4th general star (Indonesian: General Posthumous)
